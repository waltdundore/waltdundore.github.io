The iPod in the Fish Tank

One conversation. One insight. One person building with AI.

The Story

When Steve Jobs saw the first iPod prototype, engineers said it was as small as possible. He dropped it in a fish tank. Air bubbles rose.

"Those are air bubbles," he said. "That means there's space in there. Make it smaller."

What This Project Is

Schools need infrastructure automation. Not expensive consultants. Not complex tools. Simple commands that work.

One conversation with Kris Lamoureux sparked this. His insight: this could exist. So I'm building it.

With AI. I write specs. AI writes code. I test everything. We document failures and successes. All of it is open.

The Job That Doesn't Exist

I'm not a developer (I write very little code). Not a project manager (too technical). Not a consultant (building a product).

I direct AI to build infrastructure automation while maintaining safety-critical standards.

There's no job title for this yet. Most days I feel like I'm figuring it out as I go.

The Fish Tank Test

Every page. Every feature. Every explanation. I ask: Are there air bubbles?

Can I remove this and still communicate the value? If yes, it's a bubble. Remove it.

This page had 11 sections. Now it has 4. No bubbles.
